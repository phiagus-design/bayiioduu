# The Standard Picture of Gene Expression

To evaluate the BAAYIODUU framework fairly, it is necessary to establish the standard molecular biology that any candidate framework must engage with[cite: 1].

## The Chemistry of DNA
Deoxyribonucleic acid is a linear polymer constructed from four monomeric units called nucleotides[cite: 1]. 
*   These nucleotides are joined by phosphodiester bonds[cite: 1].
*   The bases pair strictly: adenine with thymine (two hydrogen bonds), and guanine with cytosine (three hydrogen bonds)[cite: 1]. 
*   The two strands run antiparallel to form the canonical B-DNA double helix, though other structural variations like A-DNA and Z-DNA exist[cite: 1].

## Transcription and Translation
The expression of a gene begins with transcription, where RNA polymerase II produces a primary RNA transcript complementary to the DNA template[cite: 1]. 
*   This transcript undergoes co-transcriptional processing, including alternative splicing, which dramatically expands the proteomic complexity derivable from a finite genome[cite: 1].
*   Translation is subsequently performed by the ribosome in the cytoplasm, utilizing the genetic code to match successive nucleotide triplets (codons) with specific amino acids[cite: 1].

## Mutation Dynamics
A mutation represents any heritable change in the nucleotide sequence of a genome[cite: 1]. 
*   The standard framework treats mutation as a stochastic process[cite: 1]. 
*   The probability distribution over possible changes is determined by physical and chemical mechanisms of DNA damage and repair, entirely independent of fitness consequences[cite: 1]. 

> **Crucial Gap:** The standard framework provides no mechanism by which mutations could be directed toward specific adaptive outcomes, nor any mechanism by which cosmic-scale phenomena could affect cellular regulation[cite: 1].
